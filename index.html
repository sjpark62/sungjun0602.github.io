What Do We Learn?

AsianLang 475 is designed for students aiming to gain a better understanding of current affairs in Korea and express their opinions clearly and consistently in formal settings using superior-level Korean. The course will focus on (a) critically engaging with current issues in Korea, (b) expressing oneâ€™s opinion in Korean in a clear, consistent, and persuasive way, and (c) thinking through problems across a variety of topics by developing hypotheses and effectively discussing them with others. Students will address controversial social, political, economical, and cultural issues in contemporary Korea by placing them into context, developing and expressing their own arguments on the issues, and participating in discussions to find effective solutions. By taking this course, students will be able to cultivate a deeper understanding of current issues in Korea and develop structured arguments they can subsequently express effectively in Korean.

SYLLABUS

Attendance

- Extremely low attendance may further lower your final grade or cause you to fail the course unless a written proof of inevitable circumstances (e.g., university athletics and band events, religious holidays, medical emergencies, etc.) is provided. If you miss classes 11 times or more, you will fail.

Brainstorming worksheet

- Worksheets will be posted on each topic section in Canvas along with the five questions that will be discussed in the first class of each topic. You will be responsible for completing the worksheet according to the requirements and bringing a hardcopy to the class. You will be asked to submit this after the class.

2 2 1 posting

- After reading each article, you will be responsible for posting 2 2 1 (2 interesting points, 2 questions, and 1 suggestion for each article) on the discussion forum at Canvas. The 2 2 1 assignment should be submitted by 11PM the day before the second class on each topic to get full credit. If submitted between 11PM and the beginning of the second class, you will only get 80% of total credit.

Writing assignments

- You will be given 7 writing assignments after each topic. Details will be provided during the semester.

Group activity

- You will be paired with classmatesfor this group activity. Each group will be responsible for preparing and leading discussion activities on any other interesting content that the group feels was not sufficiently addressed in class. Details will be provided during the semester.

Final group project

- You will be paired with classmates for this group project. The group will choose one topic and prepare a 20-minute mini-lesson and a 5-minute Q & A session for the class. You and your partner(s) will be responsible for all stages in the preparation and execution of this project. The lesson needs to have appropriate readings or audio-visual materials as well as discussion activities. Details will be provided during the semester.
